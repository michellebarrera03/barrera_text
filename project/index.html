June 12, 1842

The Night It All Happened

Written by Belle French

So many people have asked me what I felt that night — that night everything changed. The truth is, I don't think I will ever find the perfect words for it...

When I saw the last rose petal fall, my heart sank. I thought I had lost him forever. But then something incredible happened. The beast who everyone feared and judged began to change right before my eyes. His posture straightened, and suddenly, Prince Adam was standing there. It wasn't just his appearance that transformed. It felt like his heart had returned to the person he had always wanted to be.

In that moment I realized I had been right to trust him all along. People warned me to stay away — even my father worried that I was making a terrible mistake by believing there was good hiding beneath all that anger and loneliness. But I saw something different. I saw someone who was hurting, someone capable of kindness, and someone who simply needed to be heard and understood.

As I looked around the ballroom, I noticed everybody watching us. The castle staff, now free from the curse, smiled with tears in their eyes. It felt like this whole time they had been waiting for this moment. For the first time, I didn't feel strange for seeing the good in someone everyone else had given up on. I'm so glad I followed my heart.

More than anything, I felt peace. It wasn't the kind of happiness you read about in books or hear about in stories. It was scary, but I knew something was calling to me. I felt Prince Adam's heart before he heard his own. I just needed him to hear it too. And once he did, it felt like peace and harmony.

That night taught me that love isn't about perfection or appearances. Sometimes it's about believing in someone when no one else will. And in the end, following my heart gave me the happy ending I never thought I would have.

~ Belle

The Library That Holds the World

Written by Belle French

There is something sacred about a room filled with books. The smell of old leather and dried ink, the way the afternoon light falls in golden columns across the shelves — it is enough to make one believe in magic...

Each spine a doorway, each page a key. I have lived a thousand lives without ever leaving this room...

Candlelight & Quiet Evenings

Written by Belle French

When the world grows still and the candles are lit, I find my truest thoughts rising to the surface like cream. The hearth crackles softly and the clock on the mantle ticks in a rhythm that feels older than memory...

These are the hours I live for — the quiet ones, golden and unhurried, smelling of beeswax and old wood...
